Overview

WebPOS
Experience

When legacy point-of-sale systems slowed down the checkout process during peak hours, our challenge was to create an intuitive, touch-first online alternative accessible from any device in the store.

Collaborating with store managers and cashiers, we tailored our platform to craft a fast journey through the checkout process. Led by a dedicated team, we aimed to capture the essence of a modern retail experience, from scanning to receipt, while aligning with our client's branding.

Through research, innovative design, and trust-building prototypes, we transformed slow checkout lines into an opportunity to bring faster service closer to customers.

Process

Initial Challenges

I led the design and collaborated with a cross-functional team, including a Product Manager, three Engineers, and a Motion Designer. Upon joining the project, I faced the challenge of quickly demonstrating our capabilities to the team, who entrusted us with a tight timeline.

Research & Immersion

My initial focus involved extensive research to gather inspiration from existing POS experiences to identify best practices. Simultaneously, I immersed myself in the store branding, with a particular emphasis on the unique personality of their checkout flow. Collaborating with the engineers, we explored modern web technologies, evaluating which features to utilize.

Trust Building Strategy

Recognizing the team's previous difficulties with wireframes, I adopted a strategic approach by creating a high-fidelity prototype with a landing page and interactive mockups. This decision was pivotal in gaining their trust and provided opportunities for deeper exploration and discovery.

Crafting the Style Mood Board

Recognizing the brand's exacting standards, I designed a style mood board to effectively communicate my design process to the Team.

Style-tiles mood boards are very effective in showing where a design came from

First Concept

To present the first concept, I went the extra mile to prove our capability to deliver the experience expected. I designed an interactive prototype that introduced unique elements such as quick-add buttons, split payments, and fast barcode scanning simulation.

WebPOS Solution

The resulting virtual experience invites partners and customers to explore the checkout platform. Users can move at their own pace and delve deeper into the inventory management from start to finish.

Get ready for your checkout journey

Before entering the environment, users are presented with a clear interface. This page serves two primary purposes: providing context to newcomers and delivering educational content about initiatives at the store.

Explore the features & unlock extra speed

Impact

This project garnered recognition worldwide and was translated into multiple languages. It also received positive feedback from key business partners across the globe.

Integrated into Training

Key Learnings

My journey through this project provided several key insights.

Gaining Trust with High-Fidelity

While wireframes are valuable, in this case, a high-fidelity prototype proved indispensable in gaining the client's trust. This trust, in turn, allowed for deeper discovery work.

Pick Your Battles Wisely

Knowing when to make trade-offs and when to prioritize critical elements is essential, especially when collaborating with external clients and your tech team.

Be Prepared to Adapt

User testing can present surprising challenges, emphasizing the need for adaptability and quick adjustments when an unexpected situation happen.
