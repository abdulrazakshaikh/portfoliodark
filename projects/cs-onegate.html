Overview

VizLog
Visitor Management

Because a safe world begins with a safe complex. VizLog Gatekeeper was designed to modernize security and visitor management for residential and commercial spaces.

The objective was to replace cumbersome paper logs with a swift, digital solution that security personnel could use with minimal training.

High Level Goals

01

Design an incredibly intuitive interface for security guards with varying levels of digital literacy.

02

Enable sub-10-second visitor entry processing to prevent queues at the gate.

Process

Initial Challenges

Security guards often struggled with complex apps, leading to long wait times and frustrated visitors. We needed a UI that was practically foolproof.

Research & Immersion

I spent time observing security personnel at busy complex gates. We found that large buttons, high contrast, and voice-assisted inputs were critical for their workflow, especially at night or in bright sunlight.

Trust Building Strategy

We developed a bare-bones, highly focused prototype containing only the essential actions: Entry, Exit, and Alert. Testing this directly with the guards built immediate confidence.

Crafting the Style Mood Board

Recognizing the brand's exacting standards, I designed a style mood board to effectively communicate my design process to the Team.

Style-tiles mood boards are very effective in showing where a design came from

First Concept

The first concept introduced a scanning-first approach. Guards could scan a QR code from a visitor's phone to auto-fill details, bypassing manual entry entirely.

VizLog Solution

VizLog is a streamlined, robust app that empowers security personnel and keeps complexes safe.

Rapid Processing

Optimized the flow to just 3 taps for regular visitors, significantly reducing wait times.

High Visibility Design

Implemented a high-contrast theme ensuring readability under harsh sunlight and low-light night shifts.

Impact

The system drastically improved security protocol adherence and resident satisfaction.

Key Learnings

My journey through this project provided several key insights.

Gaining Trust with High-Fidelity

While wireframes are valuable, in this case, a high-fidelity prototype proved indispensable in gaining the client's trust. This trust, in turn, allowed for deeper discovery work.

Pick Your Battles Wisely

Knowing when to make trade-offs and when to prioritize critical elements is essential, especially when collaborating with external clients and your tech team.

Be Prepared to Adapt

User testing can present surprising challenges, emphasizing the need for adaptability and quick adjustments when an unexpected situation happen.
